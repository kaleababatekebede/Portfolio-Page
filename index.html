HOW I GOT HERE

About me

I trained as a medical doctor first. Years of clinical work taught me how to take a messy set of symptoms, ask the right questions, and work backward to what's actually wrong.

A habit that turned out to translate almost perfectly to debugging software. Somewhere along the way, building the tools I wished I'd had in the clinic became the thing I couldn't stop doing after hours, and eventually it became the main thing.

These days I split my attention between fullstack development and AI — building products end to end, from database schema to the interface someone actually touches, and increasingly wiring AI into that experience in ways that are useful rather than gimmicky.
